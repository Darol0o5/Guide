## Adjustability



### At-a-glance

- **Wheelbase:** ✅

- **Camber:** Front ✅ / Rear ✅ 

- **Toe:** Front ✅ / Rear ✅

- **Caster:** ❌

- **Ackermann quick adjustment:** ✅

- **Ride height:** Front ✅/ Rear ✅

- **Track width:** Front ✅/ Rear ✅

- **Front shocks:** preload ✅

- **Rear shocks:** preload ✅

- **Active systems:** ✅

- **Motor position:** mid ✅ / high ✅/ rear ✅

- **Servo position:** ✅

- **Pinion-Spur distance:** ✅

- **Front knuckle KPI hinge point:** ❌

- **Front knuckle steering linkage hinge point:** ❌

- **Steering rack linkage hinge point:** ❌

- **Extendable dogbones:** ❌




### Details

- **Wheelbase adjustment method:** *slider*

- **Wheelbase range:** *86–102 mm / up to 125 mm with wide track upgrade**

- **Track width range:** *60–68 mm(measured without wheels) / up to 85 mm with wide track upgrade*

- **Caster adjustment:** *static*

- **Ackermann adjustment:** *stepless*

- **Rear toe behavior:** *adjustable camber gain*

## Drivetrain

- **Gearbox type:** *gear-driven(plastic gears)*

- **Motor orientation:** *transverse*

- **Forces:** *anti-torque*

- **Reversible:** ✅

- **Differential:** *spool*



---



## Steering

- **Steering method:** *pivoted*

- **Steering system:** *bellcrank* 

- **Servo position:** *lower deck*



---



## Suspension

- **Front:** *double wishbone, independent, 2 shocks*

- **Rear:** *multi-link, independent, 2 shocks*

- **Shocks type:** *friction shocks*



## Notes

- You need the wide track upgrade and extendable dogbones if you plan to run it with medium-big 1/24 bodies, because of the limited stock track width and wheelbase. 

- You can DIY caster adjustment by filind down the bulkhead or arms and use shims for adjustment.
