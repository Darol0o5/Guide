# All-in-One Small Scale RWD Drift Guide

This is the beginning of a long-term project.

Small-scale RWD drifting has grown organically — through experiments, Facebook posts,
private messages, trial and error, and a lot of undocumented knowledge.
Finding reliable information often means digging through years of posts and opinions.

This guide exists to slowly bring that information into one place.

---

## What is this right now?

At this stage, this site is **not finished** and **not complete**.

The main completed section at the moment is:

### 📜 [History](history/how-rwd-came-to-small-scale.md)
A researched timeline of how RWD entered small-scale drifting —  
from early experiments to the pre-COVID era.

This was the starting point of the project and the foundation for everything else.

---

## What is planned next

The project is being built step by step. Planned sections include:

### 🧩 [Chassis Catalogue](chassis/catalogue.md)
A structured catalogue of small-scale RWD chassis:
- CNC / production kits
- 3D-printed and independent designs
- limited runs and experimental platforms

This is currently **work in progress** and expanding gradually.

---

### 🧠 Terminology 
Clear explanations of commonly misunderstood concepts:
- camber, toe, caster
- KPI, scrub radius
- pro/anti torque, pro/anti squat
- suspension and steering geometry

Some pages already exist, many more are planned.

---

### 🚀 Beginner Guides
Practical information for newcomers:
- what electronics are actually required
- compatibility pitfalls
- realistic expectations by budget
- how to choose a chassis without relying on opinions alone

---

## Why this project exists

Because the same questions are asked again and again.

Because valuable knowledge disappears in chat history.

Because newcomers deserve something better than contradictory answers.

And because small-scale RWD drifting has a history and depth worth documenting.

---

## Work in progress

Everything here is evolving.

Information will be added, corrected, refined, and expanded over time.
Some data may be incomplete or missing — especially for rare or undocumented chassis.

This is expected.
