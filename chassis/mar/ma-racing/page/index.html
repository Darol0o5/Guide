    MA Racing¶

    Quick facts¶

    • Developed by: MA Racing

    • Release: December 2021

    • Origin: China

    • Status: Discontinued

    • Production: Batch

    • Scale: 1/24

    • Body mounting: Magnet mounting

    • Materials: Aluminum, carbon fiber, (before V1.2, most parts were only 3D printed nylon and carbon fiber)

    Adjustability¶

    At-a-glance¶

    • Wheelbase: ✅

    • Track width: Front ✅ / Rear ✅ (v1.5)

    • Camber: Front ✅ / Rear ✅

    • Camber gain: Front ❌ / Rear ✅

    • Caster: ✅

    • Ackermann quick adjustment: ✅

    • Toe: Front ✅ / Rear ✅ (via toe blocks 0 or 2 degree)

    • Ride height: Front ✅ / Rear ✅

    • Front shocks: preload ✅ / angle ✅

    • Rear shocks: preload ✅ / angle ✅

    • Active systems: ❌

    • Motor position: mid ✅ / high ✅ / rear ✅ (Option parts)

    • Belt tension/Pinion size: ✅

    • Extendable dogbones: ❌ (✅ option parts)

    • Front KPI: ❌

    • Steering linkage position on knuckle: ❌

    • Servo inclination: ❌

    • Steering rack linkage position: ❌

    Details¶

    • Wheelbase adjustment method: slider

    • Wheelbase range: 98–120 mm

    • Track width range: 75–85 mm

    • Caster adjustment: shims

    • Ackermann adjustment: stepless

    • Rear toe behavior: static

    Drivetrain¶

    • Gearbox type: gear-driven

    • Motor orientation: transverse

    • Forces: anti-torque

    • Reversible: ✅

    • Differential: spool

    Steering¶

    • Steering method: direct

    • Servo position: upper deck + lower deck bracket after v1.2

    Suspension¶

    • Front: double wishbone, independent, 2 shocks

    • Rear: double wishbone, independent, 2 shocks (optional straight bridge upgrade)

    • Shocks type: friction shocks

    Notes¶

    The original MA Racing platform was the company's first chassis and marked the beginning of what would become one of the most respected names in small-scale drifting. Rather than waiting until a fully CNC-machined design was ready, MA Racing introduced a mechanically capable platform built primarily from 3D-printed nylon and carbon fiber.

    Instead of replacing the chassis with an entirely new design, the platform evolved through successive revisions that gradually introduced CNC aluminum components. Existing owners could upgrade their chassis over time, allowing both the platform and the brand to mature together.

    By late 2022, the platform had completed its transition into a premium CNC chassis widely recognized for its exceptional manufacturing quality. The reputation established by the original MA Racing platform laid the foundation for every model that followed, including the D32, D24 and DLR8..

    Evolution¶

    MA Racing V1 released in December 2021.

    The first production version that established the foundation of the MA Racing platform.

    Among the earliest teasers were bearing-mounted front suspension arms, an unusual design for the scale at the time that hinted at the company's engineering direction.

    MA Racing v1.1 upgrade

    The first CNC aluminum upgrades appeared, including the branded upper deck and aluminum spur gear.

    MA Racing V1.2 (February 2022) & V1.3 (May 2022)

    By this stage, the transition from 3D-printed nylon toward CNC aluminum was well underway.

    During this period, MA Racing also introduced optional upgrades including:

    • CNC brake caliper and disc
    • Rear motor mount
    • Rear straight bridge suspension

    V1.4 was released in July 2022.

    V1.4 was generally regarded as the platform's first "full aluminum" configuration. New aluminum battery holder, extendable rear arms and additional CNC components completed the transition from the original mixed-material platform into a premium chassis.

    The final evolution of the original platform, refining an already mature design before MA Racing moved on to the next generation. It marked the end of the platform that established the company's identity.
